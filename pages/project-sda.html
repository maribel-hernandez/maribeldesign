Snap Design Academy

Snap Design Academy is a 6-week academy for Los Angeles' Community College students to learn design from Snap employees and LA-based artists. Throughout the academy, we had the opportunity to work on a variety of different assignments that allowed our imagination to overflow and gain on-hands expierence. Below are the assignments I execuated during my time in the academy.

One Mentor, Three Icons

Each student was paired with a Snap Mentor based on our similarities and career goals. For our first assignment, we were required to interview our mentor in order to get to know them a little better. Based on our interviews we were tasked to create three icons that represented our mentors. As designers, we needed to listen, understand, and visually communicate our mentors through icons. It's Denim, It's Nature, It's My Mentor!

In addition to the three icons we created for our mentors, we needed to design four collages that translate emotions, personality, behaviors, and other qualities of human significance into elements of form through collaging.

After a couple of meetings with my mentor, I was able to learn more about his personal journey and career path. Throughout our conversations, I found that he appreciates a more simplistic and modern design approach. He shared that he enjoyed creating magazine layouts and found typography to be a great form for design. As a result, my goal for this assignment was to ensure a good layout with minimal images to represent my mentor, Joevanno.

Mural Madness Campaign

As designers it is important for us to understand and articulate a design aesthetic that draws unintentionally from historical cultural design practices to communicate a message. Therefore, using Adobe Illustrator, our task for this assignment was to make a digital mock of a design and apply an intentional design aesthetic to design an image that captures the spirit of our community via a Geofilter.

I was born and raised in Los Angeles and grew up seeing a lot of murals and art pieces all over the city. As a result, murals became my inspiration for this assignment. I wanted to find a way in which I could leverage Snapchat in order to highlight unnoticed murals in my community. My goal was to create a space for conversation with friends and showcase the very underrated artist that make L.A so beautiful with their artwork. Therefore, I decided to create a campaign called, #MuralMadness, that uses the lenses in the Snap carousel to help friends share murals in their surrounding areas. Check out the three lenses below:

SnapShops

For our final assignment, we were put in groups of three to help further a social issue cause. As a group, we were very passionate about the foster youth community. As a result, we decided to partner with Art + Practice, a local non-profit orginization based in Los Angeles that provide housing, job skills, educational resources and financial assistance to Foster Youth. Throughout our research and conversations with our partner, we found that they do extraordinary job in helping foster youth through the incredible resources they provide. However, not many people know about this organization.

Therefore, we were committed in helping Art + Pratcice with brand awareness, so that they can reach even more foster youth and/or potential donors. In addition, we wanted to leverage Snapchat in order to help with our goal. As a result we created both a physical experience and used digital to help further our mission of brand awareness.
